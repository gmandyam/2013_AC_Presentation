Leverage Your Organization’s Reasons to Join the W3C
  • You should be able to list your organization’s goals in the W3C
  • This should be able to serve as a guideline to which groups (WG’s/CG’s/BG’s/Task Forces) your org should join
  • See excerpt from QuIC’s new member presentation at 2012 AC meeting for an example …
3
QuIC’s Goals in W3C
  • Leverage open standards to make HTML5 a viable alternative to native apps for developers
  • Work with web community to reduce fracturing across mobile and desktop browsers
    • QuIC’s involvement with the Core Mobility Community Group
  • Utilize our expertise in wireless connectivity and mobile platforms to influence web standards for the following:
    • Improving page download times
    • Reducing power consumption for handheld devices while browsing the web
    • Ensuring device features are available to developers via Javascript/HTML
4
Limit your Involvement to Working Groups that you Want to Influence
  • There is a significant amount of work that takes place with spec-producing groups that are outside of the specification itself, e.g.
    • IPR background searches and commitments
    • Testing/Verification
    • Compatibility with other standards
  • Then there are the areas that of the specification that your group wants to actually influence or change
5
Limit your Involvement to Working Groups that you Want to Influence (cont.)
  • W3C specs are public domain, and every group has a public mailing list for asking questions
    • If all you are interested in is information, you don’t have to join
  • If you do join, take your commitments seriously
6
W3C Standards Work is Collaborative
  • In contrast to contribution-driven SDO’s (standards development organizations), much of W3C work is conducted via email
  • Recently other collaborative tools have also come into the picture
    • Github being a prime example
  • Some companies do not easily deal with collaborative forms of specification development
    • Educate the necessary people in your organization to avoid unpleasant surprises in the future
7
And be Willing to Learn Something
  • Many participants in the W3C are long-timers with rich backgrounds
    • They often know what they are taking about
    • They are not company shills – they are truly interested in coming up with a workable standard that improves the lives of developers and end users
  • Leverage such expertise whenever possible
